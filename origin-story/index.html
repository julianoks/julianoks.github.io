Julian grew up to be a normal human boy.
He was known to do normal human things like:
  • Eat food at McDonald's
  • Walk around
  • Laugh at dogs
One day after school, Julian's parents made him come home for a family dinner.
For dinner, they prepared the usual: a single fly encased in spider-butt-silk.

Julian said:

"I cannot eat this. Not only do I not enjoy eating these bugs, but a single fly does not contain enough nutrients to sustain a human such as myself."

After a heated argument with his parents, Julian did the unthinkable: he squashed them. In his words:

"What started as a frustrated stomp became a double spider murder.
This is one of the most regretful steps I've taken."

For many years, Julian spent his time lamenting the squashing of his parents. He even tried turning himself in, but the police said that murdering your parents is not a crime if your parents are small insects.

Julian spent the remainder of his highschool and college years grieving, advocating for spiders, and programming. Some notable qoutes of his are displayed below:
  • If only I hadn't squashed my parents, I'd have a loving family. How I wish I could go back; how I wish I could have put them in a tissue and thrown them outside. I would do anything to go back.
  • Spiders are your friends. They stay out of your way. They kill and eat the bad bugs, like mosquitos and dirty house flies. And they will love you dearly if you give them the chance. And what do they ask in return? Nothing but a quiet place in your house.
  • LISP will give you magic functional programming powers.
